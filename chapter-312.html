Chapter 312 — Fat Finger, GPIF, and a Plan

We held an OTK “shareholders’ meeting,” which meant just the two of us.

“Why buy Toyota?”
“Partnership, maybe. Like Geely buying ten percent of Daimler.”
“With Toyota?”

Japan’s firms had formed a government‑backed Auto Alliance—Toyota, Honda, Softbox, Sony, Toshiba—everyone. Toyota led it. Us teaming up with Toyota? Nearly zero chance.

“Anyway, 14.97 million shares for 14.97 million yen means one yen per share. That’s nonsense.”
“Unless someone fat‑fingered it.”

I ran through famous blunders: the 2010 flash crash; Japan 2015—63,000 shares sold for 1 yen by mistake; Seosung’s ghost‑share fiasco; Hansu Securities’ options disaster—Japan’s Supreme Court ultimately ruling that “a filled trade is final.”

Who even *has* fourteen‑point‑nine‑seven million Toyota shares to dump at once? Only one: Japan’s public pension—GPIF—with its domestic equities routed increasingly through Nishida Securities, whose ownership was tangled with the First Lady’s clan.

The plan formed.

“No pre‑bids. Looks like collusion,” I said. “We wait and strike in real time.”

We tracked disclosures. GPIF’s Toyota stake slid toward 15 million. When it dipped below… that would be our window.

* * *

I wrestled with the ethics. “It isn’t illegal. But it *is* predatory.”
“Someone will buy it if we don’t,” Taek‑gyu said.
TV blared: Japan recalled its ambassador from Seoul over the court rulings and comfort‑women issue; politicians spat venom. Protesters waved Rising Sun flags and called me a murderer on NHK.

“Still think we should worry about their feelings?” he said.

I looked at Okazaki on the screen. *If they sell cheap, we buy.*

* * *

We cleared our calendars. I pre‑filled the order ticket so one keypress would fire. Japan’s market broke for lunch; Korea’s did not. We watched both sessions, every tick.

GPIF’s stake finally fell *under* fifteen million.

“Any day now,” I said.

Days passed. We stared till our eyes burned. With twenty minutes left one afternoon, I couldn’t hold it. “Bathroom. Don’t blink.”
“Got it,” he said, finger poised over Enter.
