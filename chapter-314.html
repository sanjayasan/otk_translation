Chapter 314 — Fallout

An emergency cabinet meeting convened. GPIF CIO **Koji Namiki** sat among the ministers.

“What happened? How big is the loss?” the Prime Minister demanded.
“At the close—**2.1 trillion yen**.”
Silence.

“Where was the abnormal‑order detector?” the Finance Minister asked.
“Disabled during maintenance… and someone forgot to turn it back on.”
“The approval chain?”
“Cut from five to three steps for speed. It… also failed.”

“Who bought the shares?”
“Golden Gate. We’re confirming the end client.”

Namiki’s phone rang—Nishida. He listened, went pale. “Golden Gate *Korea*. Likely OTK Company.” Faces blanched around the room.

They couldn’t legally freeze trades without damaging market credibility. Golden Gate was American; push too hard and Washington would push back. Even if they halted trading, the buyer could relay title across affiliates.

“Even if it was a fat finger,” the Prime Minister muttered, “how did they hit *that* moment?”

* * *

After the mistaken orders were filled, Toyota drifted back to 145,000 and closed at 141,000. The Nikkei finished −1.24%. Europe wobbled. Headlines screamed: *Worst fat finger; 2.3T yen gone; pensions hit.* Forums exploded.

* * *

Hyun‑joo dragged us in. We explained time priority, size priority, and luck. “What now?” she asked.
“If we’re enemies anyway,” I said, “we do it properly.”

Ambassador Tanokura slipped back into Seoul and demanded the trades be canceled “for the sake of relations.” Prime Minister Ryu listened, then said softly, “OTK is an American company. If you wish to protest… why are you here?”
