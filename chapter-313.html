Chapter 313 — The Print

Nishida’s trader **Kimura Sugihara** juggled three monitors and endless orders. He reached for coffee—empty. Headcount had been cut “for efficiency.” Safeguards suspended for a server check hadn’t been re‑enabled. The five‑step approval flow had been trimmed to three—then failed.

He keyed: retail buys, J-Com sell at 3,250, ManoTel, and—back to the GPIF account. Toyota: ¥151,300. A thousand more to sell before the bell.

He yawned, entered a marketable order. It got approved. Then a strange thing: the resting bid stack *below* the last trade vanished in one go, and fills fired at **¥1**.

“W‑wait!” He smashed Cancel in under a second, but the log showed a torrent of small fills—then the monster: **14,970,000 at ¥1**.

His chief shook him. “What did you do?” Kimura’s mouth went dry. “I… think I made a mistake.”

The Nikkei, up earlier, was now down **4.3%**.

* * *

We stared at the screen all day. With less than twenty minutes to go, I ran to the bathroom. I was gone for a minute—two at most.

A scream. I bolted back. Taek‑gyu, eyes round as saucers, just pointed.

Our account now held **14,970,000** Toyota shares.

We looked at each other—then yelled. Cost: **¥14,970,000**. Instant PnL: well over **¥200,000,000**. Return: **15,000,000%**.

The TV flashed: (Breaking) Nikkei plunges—order error suspected!

Golden Gate’s Japan desk called Korea in a panic. Korea had swept the block. Hyun‑joo pulled up our account, went pale, then roared: “OH TAEK‑GYU!”

Tokyo’s Prime Minister’s Office. Okazaki was meeting China’s Commerce Minister Li Su‑wei. A deputy whispered in his ear: a fat finger had effectively erased ten percent of Toyota; buyer unknown. Minutes later: “Likely OTK Company.”

Li set down his tea. “I’ll return to Beijing,” he said. “You won’t be focusing on this meeting anyway.”
