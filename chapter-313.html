Chapter 313 — The Print

Nishida trader **Kimura Sugihara** fought sleep, juggling the pension book with retail flow. Coffee: empty. Staff: short. Safeguards: off. Approvals: trimmed in the name of “efficiency.”

He keyed in sells; a supervisor green‑lit. Then a cascade—resting bids vanished, and the unthinkable printed:

**14,970,000 shares of Toyota at 1 yen.**

The Nikkei flipped from green to **−4.3%** in a heartbeat.

“W‑wait!” Kimura slammed Cancel. Too late. The position was gone. He stared at the log. Lines of small fills, then the monstrous one at the end. His chief grabbed his shoulders. “What did you do?”

* * *

We’d watched all day. I’d been gone for sixty seconds.

I burst from the bathroom. “What happened?”
Taek‑gyu just pointed.

Our account now held **14,970,000** shares of Toyota.

We stared, then howled. Cost: **14.97 million yen**. Instant mark‑to‑market: over **200 million yen**. Return: **15,000,000%**.

TV banners rolled: *Breaking—Nikkei plunges; order error suspected.*

Golden Gate’s Japan desk rang Korea: the **Korea** branch had taken the block. Hyun‑joo pulled up our account, went white, then bellowed: “OH TAEK‑GYU!”

* * *

In Tokyo, Prime Minister Okazaki met China’s Commerce Minister Li Su‑wei. A deputy whispered the news: a mistaken order had evaporated roughly ten percent of Toyota.

“Buyer?”
“Not confirmed—likely OTK Company.”
Li set down his tea. “I’ll return to Beijing,” he said. “Do you really think the Prime Minister can continue the meeting like this?”
